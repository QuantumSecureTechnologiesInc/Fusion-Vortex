AbstractThe present disclosure describes "HyperCycle CQC," a cryptographic method that generates quantum-resistant key pairs without relying on traditional Number Theoretic Transforms (NTT) or Lattice-based structures (Ring-LWE). Instead, it utilises a non-linear Heisenberg-Euler chaotic map evolved over a specific "Chaos Horizon" (97 cycles) across a 64-trajectory "Hyper-Torus" topology. This approach leverages the massive parallelism of modern GPU wavefronts/warps not just for speed, but as an intrinsic component of the cryptographic diffusion layer.2. Background & Problem StatementCurrent NIST Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) standards (e.g., ML-KEM/Kyber) rely on structured lattices. These algorithms are computationally bounded by memory bandwidth due to large matrix operations and complex polynomial multiplication (NTT). Furthermore, they represent a "monoculture" of security; if a breakthrough in solving the Shortest Vector Problem (SVP) occurs, standard PQC may be compromised.3.
